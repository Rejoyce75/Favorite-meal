My Favourite Meal

The name of my favorite meal is "Breadfruit". It is known in Nigeria as African breadfruit. It is a well known delicacy in Igbo land.


Breadfruit originated in New Guinea and the Indo-Malay region and was spread throughout the vast Pacific by voyaging islanders. Europeans discovered breadfruit in the late 1500s.

Ingredients for making breadfruit

  • 6 cups of ukwa
  • Potash (akanwu) teaspoon
  • Fresh pepper (about 5)
  • Ogiri (optional) (a local ingredient)
  • Maggi (half cube)
  • Salt to taste
  • Red oil (100ml)
  • 2 cups of maize.

Method of preparing breadfruit

  1. Wash the breadfruit seeds severely with lots of water.
  2. Set a cooking pot on heat, add 5 cups of water and start cooking the ukwa (breadfruit).
  3. Once it boils, add the potash. Dissolve the potash in one cup of water, filter in the clear water and throw away the residue.
  4. Cook the corn till it is very soft.
  5. Separate the seeds from the water.
  6. Pound the pepper with mortar and pestle, add about 100ml of red oil, a half stock cube, A pinch of salt, ogiri and stir (use a garri turner, a spoon-like wood).
  7. Add the soft corn and stir
  8. Then add the soft ukwa seed then turn very well with the wooden turner and you will get the exact resemblance of the image above. Taste for salt and you are done making this African delicacy.
